<p><p>The pediatric cranial orthosis market features two main types: active and passive helmets. Active helmets are designed to apply gentle corrective pressure to reshape a child's skull actively, often incorporating adjustable components for tailored fit and effectiveness. In contrast, passive helmets provide a more static support, allowing the head to grow naturally while reducing external pressures. Both types aim to address conditions like plagiocephaly, ensuring optimal cranial development in infants while enhancing comfort and safety during use.</p></p>
