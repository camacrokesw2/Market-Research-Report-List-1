<p><p>The CMOS linear image sensor market is divided into analog and digital sensor types. Analog sensors output continuous voltage signals that represent captured light, making them suitable for applications requiring high-speed imaging with low latency, such as in industrial settings. On the other hand, digital sensors convert light into discrete digital signals, offering higher resolution and compatibility with modern digital systems. This differentiation caters to various applications, including medical imaging, machine vision, and consumer electronics, enhancing overall performance and functionality.</p></p>
