<p><p>The allergy tester market encompasses various types used to diagnose allergies. Skin prick testers involve applying allergens to the skin's surface and observing reactions. Intradermal testers inject allergens into the skin for more sensitive responses. Blood testers analyze blood samples for allergen-specific antibodies, providing an alternative to skin-based methods. Others may include patch tests or challenge tests, offering additional diagnostic approaches. Together, these methods enable healthcare providers to accurately identify allergens and inform treatment strategies for patients.</p></p>
